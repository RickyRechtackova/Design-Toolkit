Graphics / Images

Sourcing royalty-free graphics and images is crucial for designers to ensure legal compliance and avoid copyright infringement issues. By using royalty-free resources, designers can confidently incorporate high-quality visuals into their projects without risking legal repercussions. Additionally, these resources offer a cost-effective solution, saving designers time and money that would otherwise be spent on licensing fees. Furthermore, using royalty-free graphics fosters creativity and flexibility, allowing designers to focus on their artistic vision without constraints. Overall, prioritising royalty-free resources promotes ethical design practices while enhancing the visual appeal and professionalism of design projects.

So where do you find royalty-free media?

Pixabay

Pixabay offers a vast library of royalty-free images, illustrations, sounds, and videos, providing designers with diverse and high-quality media options for their projects.

Vecteezy

Vecteezy offers a broad range of royalty-free vector graphics, illustrations, and icons, providing designers with diverse, creative, high-quality assets for their projects.

Unsplash

Unsplash is a leading platform for high-quality, royalty-free photography, offering designers a vast array of stunning images to enhance their projects creatively.

Colour Palette Generator

A colour palette generator is a tool designed to assist designers in creating cohesive and visually appealing colour schemes for their projects. Using principles of colour theory, the generator generates a set of colours that complement each other well and form a harmonious palette. After colours are generated, user can click on them and the colour code will automatically copy into their clipboard.

Colour Contrast Checker

Colour contrast checking is crucial for designers as it ensures readability and accessibility for all users, particularly those with visual impairments, enhancing the overall user experience.
Unsure of whether your colours have a suitable level of contrast? Try our checker below!

What your result means:

AAA

Well done!

This means you have achieved the highest level of accessibility according to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG). Meeting this AAA standard indicates that the colour contrast between text and its background is sufficient for users with the most significant visual impairments, ensuring optimal readability and accessibility.

AA

That's Okay!

This means you have achieved a level of accessibility according to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG). Meeting the AA standard indicates that the colour contrast between text and its background is sufficient for most users, including those with mild to moderate visual impairments, ensuring readability and accessibility for a broader range of individuals.

Fail

Uh Oh!

This means that the colour contrast between text and its background does not meet the minimum accessibility standards outlined in the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG). This means that the text may be difficult to read for many users, particularly those with visual impairments, potentially leading to a poor user experience and reduced accessibility.
